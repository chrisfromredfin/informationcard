Information Cards - use case http://informationcard.net/taxonomy/term/13/0 Use cases for Information Cards en Information Cards at the EEMA Meeting in London http://informationcard.net/blog/eema-london-2009 <p><img class="image-left" src="/sites/default/files/eema-logo.gif" alt="" width="82" height="111" />The <a href="http://www.eema.org/">European e-Identity Management Association</a> (EEMA) held its annual conference in London last week, and Information Cards were a major topic. At the opening session on Thursday, 25 June, Kim Cameron of Microsoft gave an overview of claims-based identity and the role of Information Cards in a claims-based identity metasystem. He also mentioned his new white paper, <a href="http://www.identityblog.com/wp-content/images/2009/06/UserCentricIdentityMetasystem.html">Proposal for a Common Identity Framework: A User-Centric Identity Metasystem</a>, co-authored with Reinhard Posch (federal CIO for the Austrian government since 2001) and Kai Rannenberg (the <a href="http://www.m-chair.net/">T-Mobile Chair for Mobile Business and Multilateral Security</a> at <a href="http://www.uni-frankfurt.de/">Goethe University Frankfurt</a>).</p> <p>The second day of the conference featured an Identity Metasystems Roundtable, moderated by John Bradley, ICF Fellow and OASIS IDtrust Steering Committee Member, Tony Nadalin of Microsoft, and Drummond Reed, ICF Executive Director.
